About Us

The Union County Magnet High School, ranked fourth in the nation by Newsweek in 2016, is home to the Makerspace, a pioneering resource for all students to learn more about engineering. The Makerspace will feature cutting-edge technology, such as a CNC Mill and 3-D printers, and will spearhead the integration of technology high schools across the nation. It is also the design space for the nationally-ranked UCVTS Robotics Team, Team 1257, who have been placed at the national FRC Robotics World Championship.

FAQ

  • A makerspace is an area where students are given the tools and resources needed to build, experiment, and tinker with ideas.

  • The purpose of the makerspace is to provide a hands-on opportunity for students to apply technical skills in a traditional design space.

  • All tools in the Makerspace are only dangerous if they are used incorrectly. Before using any tool, all students must read the safety manual and will be shown how to use the tools by a technology teacher. For a copy of the safety manual, please contact a technology teacher through the Meet the Team page.
