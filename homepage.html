About Us

The Union County Magnet High School, ranked fourth in the nation by Newsweek in 2014, is home to the Makerspace, a pioneering resource for all students to learn more about engineering. The Makerspace will feature cutting-edge technology, such as a CNC Mill and 3-D printers, and will be used as a pioneering resource for high schools across the nation. It is the design space for the nationally-ranked UCVTS Robotics Team, Team 1257, who have been placed at the national FRC Robotics World Championship.

FAQ

Q: What is a makerspace?

  • A: A makerspace is an area where students are given the tools and resources needed to build, experiment, and tinker with ideas.


    • Q: What is the purpose of the makerspace?

  • A: The purpose of the makerspace is to provide a hands-on opportunity for students to apply the skills learned through a traditional learning environment.


    • Q: Is the makerspace safe?

  • A: All tools in the Makerspace are only dangerous if they are used incorrectly. Before using any tool, all students must read the safety manual and will be shown how to use the tools by a technology teacher. For a copy of the safety manual, please contact a technology teacher through the Meet the Team page.


    • Q: Is the makerspace open to the general public?

  • A: Although the MHS Makerspace is currently open to only students, we hope to have a way for members of the community to use the space! Follow us on Twitter, Facebook, or Instagram to find out when we do!


    • Q: What safety precautions are taken?

  • A: All students have been taught general safety precautions and have been trained to use the equipment by our technology teachers.
